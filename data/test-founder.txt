Building a startup is hard. You need to find product-market fit, build a team, 
and iterate quickly. The key is to talk to customers constantly and ship fast. 
Don't overthink it - just build something people want.

As a founder, your job is to make sure the company survives. That means being 
ruthless about priorities. Focus on the one metric that matters most. Everything 
else is a distraction. Move fast and break things, but talk to users first.

The best founders are problem solvers. They see opportunities where others see 
obstacles. They're resilient, adaptable, and willing to pivot when needed. But 
they also know when to double down on what's working.
